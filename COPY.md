## Teach Page

### Header
Learn something you didn't think you could.

### Subhead
I've spent years finding the angle from which a hard idea becomes obvious. If you're already curious but haven't found the right explanation yet — that's exactly who I teach.

### Teaching Area Cards

**Mathematical Art**
Iterative transformation graphing — the math behind fractal and generative images. We start with a single function, apply it repeatedly, and watch structure emerge from chaos. Hands-on with ITGrapher, the renderer I built for exactly this purpose. No prior math background required beyond curiosity.

**Composition & Songwriting**
Music theory applied to actual writing — not theory for its own sake. Voice leading, harmonic motion, formal structure, reharmonization. Whether you're writing hymns, jazz standards, or something that doesn't have a name yet, the tools are the same.

**Mathematics**
The math that shows up in the real world: number theory, geometry, the patterns underneath things. I teach from first principles and I don't skip the interesting parts. Suitable for people who were told they weren't math people — I've never believed that.

**Programming**
How to think computationally, and how to turn that thinking into working code. I've taught beginners and worked alongside senior engineers. The starting point is always the same: what problem are we actually solving?

**Use of AI**
Practical AI literacy for people who want to use these tools well — not just use them. Prompting, agents, automation, and the judgment to know when AI helps and when it doesn't. Especially useful for educators, creatives, and nonprofit professionals.

### Bio

Andrew Springman is a composer, visual artist, educator, and software developer based in Harrisburg, NC.

His work doesn't fit neatly into one category — and he's stopped trying to make it. He composes music daily, teaches math and music theory, makes generative mathematical art, builds software tools, and has been exploring the creative applications of artificial intelligence since before it was mainstream. The common thread is making sophisticated ideas accessible — not by simplifying them, but by finding the angle from which they become inevitable.

He built ITGrapher — a real-time Metal renderer for iterative transformation fractals — as both a studio tool and a teaching instrument. The mathematical art it produces is what happens when a lifelong interest in the structure of things meets a programming environment fast enough to explore it in real time.

By day, Andrew serves as Senior Manager of Enterprise Development at the Billy Graham Evangelistic Association. His personal creative work — the compositions, the mathematical art, the teaching — happens in the hours that belong to him.

He is a Jesus follower, a husband, and a father.
